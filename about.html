About

I'm a software engineer focused on networking and distributed systems. For the last few years I've worked on routing infrastructure at a multi-cloud networking startup — owning a high-scale route-visualization pipeline (live routing state from a fleet of nodes, through durable storage, into a cache-compute layer that serves customer APIs) and working down into the lower layers: BGP/FRR internals, node-level routing services, upgrade validation, and CI/CD across a fleet of services.

My center of gravity is systems that have to stay both fast and correct at scale — hundreds of tenants, tens of thousands of routes each, freshness measured in minutes, and no good excuse for an outage. A lot of the work I'm proudest of is the kind where the win comes from restructuring so the system does less, rather than doing the same thing faster.

Alongside the product work I build tooling that multiplies the team — debugging agents, on-call automation, and internal developer infrastructure. I also mentor a more junior engineer.

What I work with

  • Languages: Go, Python, Bash
  • Networking: BGP, FRR/Zebra internals, route policy, multi-cloud routing
  • Systems: Kubernetes, AWS, Redis, S3, distributed pipelines at scale

Before that

I studied at the University of Notre Dame, where the work that's aged best was low-level systems and networking — an FTP-over-TCP implementation in C, and a from-scratch distributed blockchain. Earlier still: a Division I swimmer.
